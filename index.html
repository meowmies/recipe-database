Dinner Mexican Side Dish Vegetarian
Authentic Mexican Rice
Arroz Rojo — as taught by Abuela
Prep
5 min
Cook
30 min
Total
35 min
Serves
5
Ingredients
1½ cupsextra long grain white rice
3 tbspneutral oil
¼onion, diced
1 clovegarlic, crushed
1 tbspKnorr chicken bouillon
½ tspgarlic powder
2roma tomatoes (~8 oz)
2 cupshot water
¼ tspsalt, to taste
3 sprigscilantro
1jalapeño
Instructions
01
Heat oil in a heavy-bottomed pan over medium. Fry the diced onion and crushed garlic until lightly browned. Remove the garlic clove and set aside.
02
Add rice to the pan with the onions. Toast in the oil, stirring constantly, for about 5 minutes until slightly puffy and golden.
03
Add chicken bouillon and garlic powder. Mix and continue toasting for one more minute.
04
Blend the roma tomatoes with the fried garlic clove to make about 1 cup (8 oz) of tomato sauce.
05
Add the tomato sauce to the rice, stirring constantly for 30 seconds until the color deepens to a warm reddish-orange. Watch the heat to avoid burning.
06
Pour in the HOT water — liquid should be bubbling on the sides of the pot when you pour it in. Stir in salt to taste.
07
Place cilantro and jalapeño on top. Boil on medium-low until water partially evaporates, then reduce to low, cover, and cook 20 minutes. Do not lift the lid.
08
Turn off heat and fluff with a fork, folding in the tomato layer from the top. For softer rice, cover for an additional 10 minutes off heat.
Abuela's tipThe hot water is non-negotiable — it ensures each grain stays fluffy and separate. If the sides aren't bubbling when you pour it in, heat the water more first.
