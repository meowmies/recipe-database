Dinner Mexican Side Dish Vegetarian
Authentic Mexican Rice
Arroz Rojo — as taught by Abuela
Prep
5 min
Cook
30 min
Total
35 min
Serves
5
Scale Recipe
1×
Ingredients
1½ cupsextra long grain white rice
3 tbspneutral oil
¼onion, diced
1 clovegarlic, crushed
1 tbspKnorr chicken bouillon
½ tspgarlic powder
2roma tomatoes (~8 oz)
2 cupshot water
¼ tspsalt, to taste
3 sprigscilantro
1jalapeño
Instructions
01
Heat oil in a heavy-bottomed pan over medium. Fry the diced onion and crushed garlic until lightly browned. Remove the garlic clove and set aside.
02
Add rice to the pan with the onions. Toast in the oil, stirring constantly, for about 5 minutes until slightly puffy and golden.
03
Add chicken bouillon and garlic powder. Mix and continue toasting for one more minute.
04
Blend the roma tomatoes with the fried garlic clove to make about 1 cup (8 oz) of tomato sauce.
05
Add the tomato sauce to the rice, stirring constantly for 30 seconds until the color deepens to a warm reddish-orange. Watch the heat to avoid burning.
06
Pour in the HOT water — liquid should be bubbling on the sides of the pot when you pour it in. Stir in salt to taste.
07
Place cilantro and jalapeño on top. Boil on medium-low until water partially evaporates, then reduce to low, cover, and cook 20 minutes. Do not lift the lid.
08
Turn off heat and fluff with a fork, folding in the tomato layer from the top. For softer rice, cover for an additional 10 minutes off heat.
Abuela's tipThe hot water is non-negotiable — it ensures each grain stays fluffy and separate. If the sides aren't bubbling when you pour it in, heat the water more first.

Dessert Cookies Baking
Levain Bakery Chocolate Chip Cookies
Thick, gooey copycat — by Melissa Stadler
Prep
10 min
Cook
11 min
Total
21 min
Makes
8
Scale Recipe
1×
Ingredients
1 cupcold butter, cut into small cubes
1 cupbrown sugar
½ cupsugar
2eggs
1½ cupscake flour*
1½ cupsall-purpose flour
1 tspcornstarch
¾ tspbaking soda
¾ tspsalt
2 cupschocolate chips
2 cupswalnuts, roughly chopped
Instructions
01
Preheat oven to 410°F.
02
In a large mixing bowl, cream together cold cubed butter, brown sugar, and sugar for 4 minutes until creamy.
03
Add eggs one at a time, mixing well after each one.
04
Stir in both flours, cornstarch, baking soda, and salt. Mix until just combined — do not overmix. Fold in chocolate chips and walnuts.
05
Divide into 8 large balls and place on a lightly colored cookie sheet — only 4 per sheet, they're big!
06
Bake for 9–12 minutes until golden brown on top. Let rest for at least 10 minutes before eating so they can set up.
Key tipsCold butter is essential — don't let it soften. The high oven temp (410°F) cooks the outside fast and keeps the inside gooey. Cake flour can be subbed with all-purpose. Add 1 tsp vanilla with the eggs if you like, though the original Levain recipe skips it.

Dinner Mexican Side Dish Vegan Instant Pot
Instant Pot Refried Beans
From dried beans, from scratch
Prep
10 min
Cook
60 min
Total
70 min
Serves
4–6
Scale Recipe
1×
Beans
8 ozdried pinto beans, sorted & rinsed
2 clovesgarlic, whole with skins on
½ tspfine sea salt
¼ tspbaking soda
1bay leaf
3 cupswater
Aromatics
¼ cupvegetable oil
1 mediumonion, minced
2 clovesgarlic, smashed
1jalapeño, minced
1½ tspfine sea salt (total)
Instructions
01
Add the beans, whole garlic cloves, ½ tsp salt, baking soda, bay leaf, and water to your Instant Pot. Lock the lid and pressure cook on High for 40 minutes, then quick release. Discard the bay leaf and garlic. Reserve all beans and cooking liquid.
02
Heat vegetable oil in a large frypan over medium-high until shimmering. Add the minced onion, smashed garlic, jalapeño, and ½ tsp salt. Sauté until onions are browned at the edges, about 5 minutes.
03
Add the beans and all their cooking liquid to the frypan — careful, the hot oil may splatter. Sprinkle 1 tsp salt over the beans. Mash and stir, distributing the onions evenly throughout.
04
Keep cooking, mashing and stirring occasionally, until the beans thicken to your liking — about 8 minutes. Serve and enjoy!
TipsFor smooth beans, use a food processor instead of a masher. No Instant Pot? Simmer covered on the stovetop for 2 hours instead. Want cowboy-style? Swap vegetable oil for bacon fat. Skip the jalapeño for kid-friendly beans, or use a serrano for extra heat.

Dinner Beef Comfort Food Soup
30 Minute Chili
Brian Lagerstrom
Prep
10 min
Cook
20 min
Total
30 min
Serves
6–8
Scale Recipe
1×
Meat
2.5 lbs80/20 ground beef
Aromatics
1 largewhite onion, small diced
5–6 clovesgarlic, pressed
drizzleolive oil
Tomatoes
2 cansdiced tomatoes (14.5 oz each), drained
1 cantomato sauce (15 oz)
1 canfire-roasted tomatoes (28 oz)
2chipotle peppers in adobo
3 tbsptomato paste
Spices & Sauce
½ cupchili powder
¼ cuppaprika
1½ tbspground cumin
1 tspchili flakes
4 tsphot sauce (Frank's)
2 tbspbrown sugar
2 tbspWorcestershire sauce
1 tbspcider vinegar
1½ tbspbeef bouillon paste (Better Than Bouillon)
2 tspsalt
4 canskidney beans (16 oz each), drained
Instructions
01
Spread ground beef flat on a sheet tray and broil on high, 8" below the broiler, for 8–10 minutes until browned on top. Set aside to cool.
02
While beef broils: dice the onion, press the garlic, and drain the liquid from the diced tomatoes. Use an immersion blender to blend the fire-roasted tomatoes with the chipotle peppers until smooth.
03
Heat a drizzle of olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion, garlic, and a pinch of salt. Sweat until softened, about 5 minutes.
04
Add chili powder, paprika, cumin, and chili flakes. Stir and toast the spices for 20–30 seconds.
05
Add the blended tomato mixture, tomato sauce, drained diced tomatoes, and tomato paste. Stir and scrape the bottom.
06
Add hot sauce, brown sugar, Worcestershire, cider vinegar, beef bouillon paste, and salt. Stir to combine and simmer 5 minutes.
07
Crumble the cooled beef into small pieces and add to the pot along with the drained kidney beans. Simmer at least 10 minutes. Add water or stock if too thick.
08
Taste and adjust seasoning with salt, vinegar, brown sugar, or hot sauce. Serve topped with diced onion, sour cream, shredded cheddar, and oyster crackers.
Key tipBroiling the beef on a sheet tray instead of browning it in the pot gives you better colour and saves time — don't skip it. The chipotle-blended tomato base is where most of the depth comes from.

Dinner Mexican Beef Braise
Carne Guisada
A deeply flavored braise — Texas & Mexico style
Prep
30 min
Braise
3–4 hrs
Total
4.5 hrs
Serves
8
Scale Recipe
1×
Braising Cut
2 lbschuck roast, cubed
2 lbsbeef cheeks, cubed
~1 tbspsalt (for seasoning)
Aromatic Base & Spices
drizzleneutral oil
½onion, diced
1green bell pepper, diced
1–2poblano peppers, diced
1 tspcumin
1 tspchili powder
1 tspsmoked paprika
½ tspfresh oregano
1bay leaf
Braising Liquid
~3 cupsbeef broth (enough to submerge)
1 candiced tomatoes (small)
as neededcornstarch slurry (to thicken)
Instructions
01
Preheat oven to 300°F / 150°C. Add a drizzle of neutral oil to a Dutch oven over medium heat. Sauté the onion, bell pepper, and poblano until soft and translucent. Add the cumin, chili powder, smoked paprika, and oregano and bloom the spices for 30 seconds. Remove the aromatic base and set aside.
02
Increase heat to medium-high. Trim any excess fat from the beef cheeks. Add all the cubed meat to the pot and season evenly with salt. Sear until browned — work in batches if you want deeper color and flavor.
03
Return the aromatic base to the pot. Add the diced tomatoes and enough beef broth to just barely submerge the meat. Tuck in the bay leaf.
04
Cover and braise in the oven for 1 hour. Remove the lid to encourage browning and evaporation. Continue braising uncovered for another 2–3 hours, until the meat is completely tender and the liquid has reduced to a rich sauce.
05
Return the pot to the stovetop. If the sauce needs thickening, bring to a simmer and stir in a cornstarch slurry a little at a time until you hit your desired consistency. Taste and adjust seasoning.
06
Serve with Mexican rice, refried beans, warm flour tortillas, queso fresco, and sliced avocado. Leftovers are incredible in quesadillas, tacos, and tortas.
TipsUsing half chuck roast and half beef cheeks gives extra richness — but all chuck works great too. The longer the braise, the better. Don't rush step 4. For extra depth, sear the meat in small batches so each cube gets proper contact with the pan.
